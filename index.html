1.3 Data errors

Two types of errors are considered at the moment:

  1. Missing data.

  2. Outlier values out of reasonable range, i.e., which have a distance from the median that is farther than 1.5 times the inter quartile range.

1.3.1 Missing values

Overall, there are 647 variables with some missing data (out of 649) for a total number of 22425518 missing entries in the whole dataset (88.43% of missingness).

1.3.2 Possible errors into the data

For numerical variables, outliers are considered possible errors, i.e. values farther than 1.5 times the inter quartile range from the median.

Overview

In Italy there are 23 medical surgical (M/S) Pediatric Intensive Care Units (PICUs):

  • 11 in the north
  • 8 in the center
  • 4 in the south

of the country.

Data reported here analyze patients admitted in PICUs from 1992 to 2021.

Only children 0 – 16 years old who have a severity score on admission which allow to estimate a Standardized Mortality Ratio (SMR) were considered.

Exclusion criteria were “preterm or > 16 years old,” or not completed data entry

4 Chronic Complex Conditions (CCC) on Admissions

Overview

Chronic complex condition definition was taken by the paper of Feudtner C et al: “Pediatric Deaths Attributable to Complex Chronic Conditions: A Population-Based Study of Washington State, 1980-1997” published in Pediatrics in 2000 (106:205-209) and was defined as “any medical condition that can be reasonably expected to last at least 12 months, unless death intervenes, and to involve either one or more different organ systems.” CCC were divided within 10 categories (defined macrodiagnosis): neurologic, neuromuscular, cardiologic, oncoematologic, respiratory, syndromic, renal, metabolic, malformation, other. Moreover, the system allows to record the specific chronic disease

5 Severity on admission and outcome

Overview

The registry adopted, since the beginning, the Pediatric Index of Mortality (PIM) score. The PIM2 variables are recorded and the risk of death is automatically calculated by the system. In 2013, after a revision of the coefficient of the variables, the new PIM3 score was published. We updated the PIM page with the new formula and since 2014 we calculate both the 2 and 3 version. Moreover, retrospectically, we collected all the new variables and through the new coefficients we derived the PIM3 score for all the patient in the registry.

The Standardized Mortality Ratio (SMR) was estimated as the ratio between observed deaths/expected deaths. Expected deaths were derived as the sum of all the PIM scores for each category considered.

The new score seems to perform better than the old one, with a more adequate estimation of deaths and a more correct SMR.
